INTRODUCTION
At Walabu Travel ("Walabu Travel", "we", "us" or "our"), we are committed to protecting your privacy and safeguarding your personal information.

Just as our staff regard their privacy as paramount, your privacy is very important to us at Walabu Travel. With that in mind, we have established information handling practices for Walabu Travel that we believe are consistent with the highest standards and best practices of organisations doing business on the Internet, as well as complying with relevant legislation.

This privacy policy (the "Policy" or "Privacy Policy") applies to the collection, use and disclosure of Personal Information by Walabu Travel, including the practices that apply when using our website ("our website" or "our site"), including, specifically, the information we collect about you, when and how we collect that information and what may happen to that information.

"Personal Information" is any information that is identifiable with you, as an individual. Personal Information, however, does not include certain business contact information.

Walabu Travel has prepared this detailed Privacy Policy because we believe you should know as much as possible about our practices so that you can make an informed decision about the extent of your use of our facilities.

This Privacy Policy applies to any use of the Website, any use of the Services and any Bookings made from the United States. We may determine your location including through use of your IP address and/or the Personal Information you provide to us.

SIMPLY STATED
We will always collect your Personal Information by fair and lawful means (for example, when you register with us). We may collect Personal Information from you directly and/or from third parties, where we have obtained your consent to do so or as otherwise required or permitted by law.
Walabu Travel will not disclose your Personal Information to third parties unless you have authorized us to do so, or if we are required to do so by law.
Walabu Travel will allow you to view, correct or remove your Personal Information upon written request and in accordance with applicable law.
Walabu Travel will take reasonable steps to protect the security of the Personal Information we collect from you.

COOKIES AND PIXEL TAGGING
Walabu Travel, or a third-party advertiser we link to, may place a "cookie" or "pixel tag" on your personal computer when you visit the site or visit a site on which we advertise.

These devices are small computer files that our computer sends to your computer and that your computer sends back to us each time you make a request from our site.

These devices are used to collect data to help us personalize and improve your experiences on web sites. The data on your use of a web site may be linked to the Personal Information you have chosen to share with us when you register. The information collected with these devices may be used in order to adequately establish the price of our offers.

Most Internet browsers automatically accept cookies.

However, you can instruct your browser, by editing its options, to stop accepting cookies or prompt you before accepting a cookie from the sites you visit.

If you decide not to accept our cookies, your ability to use some features on some web site may be impaired.

You can learn more about the use of cookies, pixel tags and other devices, the information gathering practices of our network-advertising partners, and about opting out, by visiting https://networkadvertising.org.

NOTICE OF PRIVACY RIGHTS TO CALIFORNIA RESIDENTS
California law requires certain businesses to respond to requests from California users who ask about business practices related to disclosing Personally Identifiable Information to third parties for direct marketing purposes. The California "Shine the Light" law further requires us to allow California residents to opt out of certain disclosures of Personally Identifiable Information to third parties for their direct marketing purposes.

Additionally, please be aware that beginning on January 1, 2020, pursuant California Civil Code Section 1798, et seq., known as the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), all consumers in California will have the right to request that a business which collects a consumer's personal information disclose to that consumer the categories and specific pieces of personal information the business has collected. Additionally, the CCPA grants a consumer the right to request that a business that sells the consumer's personal information, or that discloses it for a business purpose, disclose to that consumer: (1) the categories of personal information that the business collected about the consumer; (2) the categories of personal information that the business sold about the consumer and the categories of third parties to whom the personal information was sold, by category or categories of personal information for each third party to whom the personal information was sold; and (3) the categories of personal information that the business disclosed about the consumer for a business purpose. Finally, the CCPA grants a consumer the right, at any time, to direct a business that sells personal information about the consumer to third parties not to sell the consumer's personal information (i.e., an "opt out" right). The CCPA sets out fairly detailed guidelines about how a company must communicate to consumers the right to opt out, how those companies can de-identify the data they seek to collect to work within the CCPA, and numerous other details about the safeguarding and treatment of the at-issue data.
